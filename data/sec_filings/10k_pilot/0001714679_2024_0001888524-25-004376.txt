<p style="margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-top:10.0pt;"><font face="Times New Roman" lang=EN-US style="font-size:9.0pt;">On November 30, 2017, DBTCA was added as a defendant to a
case brought by Commerzbank AG (&#8220;Commerzbank&#8221;) in the U.S. District Court for
the Southern District of New York, in which Commerzbank previously alleged
incorrectly that Deutsche Bank National Trust Company (&#8220;DBNTC&#8221;) served as
trustee for all 50 of the trusts at issue.&nbsp; On November 30, 2017,
Commerzbank filed a second amended complaint that names DBTCA as a defendant in
addition to DBNTC.&nbsp; DBTCA serves as trustee for 1 of the 50 trusts at
issue.&nbsp; DBNTC serves as trustee for the other 49 trusts at issue.&nbsp;
Commerzbank&#8217;s second amended complaint brings claims for violation of the U.S.
Trust Indenture Act of 1939 (&#8220;TIA&#8221;); breach of contract; breach of fiduciary
duty; negligence; violation of the New York Streit Act (&#8220;Streit Act&#8221;); and
breach of the covenant of good faith.&nbsp; However, in the second amended
complaint, Commerzbank acknowledges that, before DBTCA was added to the case,
the court dismissed Commerzbank&#8217;s TIA claims for the trusts governed by pooling
and servicing agreements, as well as its Streit Act claims and claims for
breach of the covenant of good faith, and Commerzbank only includes these
claims to preserve any rights on appeal.&nbsp; The second amended complaint
alleges that DBNTC and DBTCA caused Commerzbank to suffer &#8220;hundreds of millions
of dollars in losses,&#8221; but the complaint does not include a demand for money
damages in a sum certain.&nbsp; On January 29, 2018, DBNTC and DBTCA filed an
answer to the second amended complaint.&nbsp; On December 7, 2018, DBNTC and
DBTCA filed a motion for summary judgment.&nbsp; Also on December 7, 2018,
Commerzbank, jointly with the Phoenix Light plaintiffs, filed a motion for
partial summary judgment.&nbsp; On February 8, 2022, the court issued an order
in which it granted in part DBNTC and DBTCA&#8217;s motion for summary judgment and
denied plaintiffs&#8217; motion for partial summary judgment.&nbsp; As a result of
that order, many of plaintiffs&#8217; claims and theories were dismissed with
prejudice.&nbsp; On September 26, 2024, DBNTC and DBTCA filed a motion for
summary judgment, which has been fully briefed.</font></p>

<p style="margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-top:10.0pt;"><font face="Times New Roman" lang=EN-US style="font-size:9.0pt;">On December 30, 2015, IKB International, S.A. in Liquidation
and IKB Deutsche Industriebank A.G. (collectively, &#8220;IKB&#8221;), as an investor in 37
RMBS trusts, filed a summons with notice in the Supreme Court of the State of
New York, New York County, against DBNTC and DBTCA as trustees of the trusts.
On May 27, 2016, IKB served its complaint asserting claims for breach of
contract, breach of fiduciary duty, breach of duty to avoid conflicts of
interest, violation of the Streit Act, violation of the TIA, violation of
Regulation AB, and violation of Section 9 of the Uniform Commercial Code. IKB
alleges that DBNTC and DBTCA are liable for over U.S. $268 million in damages.
On October 5, 2016, DBNTC and DBTCA, together with several other trustees
defending lawsuits by IKB, filed a joint motion to dismiss. On January 6, 2017,
IKB filed a notice of discontinuance, voluntarily dismissing with prejudice all
claims as to three trusts. On June 20, 2017, the parties filed a stipulation,
voluntarily dismissing with prejudice all claims as to four additional trusts.
On January 27, 2021, the court granted in part and denied in part DBNTC and
DBTCA&#8217;s motion to dismiss. The court granted the motion to dismiss with respect
to IKB&#8217;s claims for violations of the Streit Act, Regulation AB, and Section 9
of the Uniform Commercial Code, as well as certain aspects of IKB&#8217;s claims for
breach of contract, breach of fiduciary duty, and violation of the TIA. The
court denied the remainder of the motion to dismiss. IKB&#8217;s remaining claims for
breach of contract, breach of fiduciary duty, breach of duty to avoid conflicts
of interest, and violation of the TIA will proceed. On May 10, 2021, DBNTC and
DBTCA filed a notice of appeal with the New York Supreme Court Appellate
Division, First Department, regarding certain aspects of the court&#8217;s order on
the motion to dismiss.&nbsp; On May 20, 2021, IKB filed a notice of cross
appeal with respect to other aspects of that order. On August 30, 2022, the New
York Supreme Court, Appellate Division, First Department affirmed in part and
reversed in part the court&#8217;s order on the motion to dismiss.&nbsp; After DBNTC
and DBTCA appealed the First Department&#8217;s decision, on June 15, 2023, the New
York Court of Appeals reversed the First Department&#8217;s decision in part, dismissing
certain additional contract claims, as well as IKB&#8217;s claims for breach of
fiduciary duty and breach of duty to avoid conflicts of interest.&nbsp; On June
2, 2021, IKB filed a motion for re-argument regarding certain aspects of the
court&#8217;s order on the motion to dismiss, which the court denied on August 3,
2021.&nbsp; On May 13, 2021, DBNTC and DBTCA filed an answer to the
complaint.&nbsp; On October 28, 2021, the parties filed a stipulation,
voluntarily dismissing with prejudice all claims as to seven additional
trusts.&nbsp; On December 29, 2021, the parties filed a stipulation,
voluntarily dismissing with prejudice all claims as to one additional
trust.&nbsp; On April 22, 2022, the parties filed a stipulation, voluntarily
dismissing with prejudice all claims as to 17 certificates at issue, including
all claims as to 5 trusts.&nbsp; On February 28, 2023, the parties filed a
stipulation, voluntarily dismissing with prejudice all claims as to two trusts,
leaving 15 trusts at issue.&nbsp; On November 21, 2023, the parties filed a stipulation,
voluntarily dismissing with prejudice all claims as to three trusts, leaving 12
trusts at issue.&nbsp; On November 14, 2024, DBNTC and DBTCA filed a motion for
summary judgment.&nbsp; Also on November 14, 2024, IKB filed a motion for
partial summary judgment.&nbsp; Those motions are being briefed.</font></p>

<p style="margin-bottom:0in;margin-left:0in;margin-right:0in;margin-top:10.0pt;"><font face="Times New Roman" lang=EN-US style="font-size:9.0pt;">In December 2014, Phoenix Light SF Limited (Phoenix Light)
