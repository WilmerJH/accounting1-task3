<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><U>ITEM 1.</U> <U>BUSINESS</U>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">DaimlerChrysler Auto Trust 2004-B (the &#147;Trust&#148;) was formed under the laws of Delaware pursuant to
an Amended and Restated Trust Agreement dated as of July&nbsp;1, 2004, among DaimlerChrysler Retail
Receivables LLC (&#147;DCRR&#148;), DaimlerChrysler Services North America LLC (&#147;DCS&#148; and &#147;Servicer&#148;), and
Deutsche Bank Trust Company Delaware, acting thereunder not in its individual capacity but solely
as trustee of the Trust.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">On July&nbsp;30, 2004, the Trust issued $380,000,000 aggregate principal amount of 1.72% Asset Backed
Notes, Class&nbsp;A-1 (the &#147;Class&nbsp;A-1 Notes&#148;), $375,000,000 aggregate principal amount of 2.48% Asset
Backed Notes, Class&nbsp;A-2 (the &#147;Class&nbsp;A-2 Notes&#148;), $480,000,000 aggregate principal amount of 3.18%
Asset Backed Notes, Class&nbsp;A-3 (the &#147;Class&nbsp;A-3 Notes&#148;), and $220,000,000 aggregate principal amount
of 3.71% Asset Backed Notes, Class&nbsp;A-4 (the &#147;Class&nbsp;A-4 Notes&#148; and together with the Class&nbsp;A-1
Notes, the Class&nbsp;A-2 Notes and the Class&nbsp;A-3 Notes, the &#147;Class&nbsp;A Notes&#148;), and $45,000,000 aggregate
principal amount of 3.89% Class&nbsp;B Notes (the &#147;Class&nbsp;B Notes&#148; and, together with the Class&nbsp;A Notes,
the &#147;Notes&#148;). The Notes were issued pursuant to an Indenture dated as of July&nbsp;1, 2004, between the
Trust and JPMorgan Chase Bank, (the &#147;Indenture Trustee&#148;). The assets of the Trust include a pool
of motor vehicle retail installment sale contracts (&#147;Receivables&#148;), secured by security interests
in the motor vehicles financed thereby and including certain monies due or received thereunder on
or after July&nbsp;14, 2004, transferred to the Trust by DCS on July&nbsp;30, 2004. The Notes are secured by
the assets of the Trust pursuant to the Indenture. The Class&nbsp;A-1 Notes were not offered publicly.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Trust has no employees.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Securities and Exchange Commission maintains an Internet site that contains reports, proxy and
information statements, and other information regarding issuers that file electronically with the
SEC. The address of the SEC Internet site is <U>http://www.sec.gov</U>.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The Internet address for obtaining free of charge the Trust&#146;s annual report on Form&nbsp;10-K and
current reports on Form&nbsp;8-K and amendments (if any) filed or furnished pursuant to Section&nbsp;13(a)
and 15(d) of the Exchange Act as soon as reasonably practicable after such material is
electronically filed with the SEC is <U>http://investor.chryslerfinancial.com</U>.

<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><U>NOTE 1 &#150; SUMMARY OF SIGNIFICANT ACCOUNTING POLICIES</U>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Basis of Accounting</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">The financial statements of DaimlerChrysler Auto Trust 2004-B (the &#147;Trust&#148;) are prepared on the
basis of cash receipts and disbursements, which is a comprehensive basis of accounting other than
accounting principles generally accepted in the United States of America. The primary difference
from the accrual basis to this basis is that the financial statements do not record provisions for
credit losses on Receivables, accrued interest receivable on the Receivables, or accrued interest
payable on the Asset Backed Notes.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Cash and Cash Equivalents</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Short-term instruments with a maturity of less than three months when purchased are considered to
be cash equivalents. The Trust received certain cash deposits from DaimlerChrysler Services North
America LLC (&#147;DCS&#148; and &#147;Servicer&#148;), which are held as liquidity and credit enhancement reserves and
invested in short-term instruments. Under the Sale and Servicing Agreement dated as of July&nbsp;1,
2004, the servicer is required to convey principal and interest collections to the Trust on a
monthly basis except when commingling conditions cease to be met under the Sale and Servicing
Agreement. During 2004, the commingling conditions set forth in the Sale and Servicing Agreement
were not met, and as a result, DCS is required to convey principal and interest collections to the
Trust on a daily basis.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Amounts Held for Future Distribution</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">Amounts Held for Future Distribution represent certain short-term investments held for future
distributions to Noteholders and for liquidity and credit enhancement reserves. Amounts held for
liquidity and credit enhancement reserves which are not utilized for future distributions to
Noteholders will be distributed to DaimlerChrysler Retail Receivables LLC (&#147;DCRR&#148;). Also included
in the balance of Amounts Held for Future Distribution are $55.2&nbsp;million of collections of
principal and interest on the Receivables. These amounts have not been applied as of the balance
sheet date to either the outstanding principal balance of the Receivables or the Asset Backed
Notes, but will be applied in accordance with the Prospectus Supplement for the next scheduled
Distribution Date.



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt"><B>Servicing</B>



<P align="left" style="font-size: 10pt">DCS services the Receivables in the Trust for an annual fee of 1% of the outstanding receivables
balance.
